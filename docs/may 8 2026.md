## 1. Purpose and Context of the Meeting

This kickoff meeting launched the **ASA Biopharm AI/ML Working Group subteam focused on agentic AI workflows** for clinical trial analysis and reporting. The group’s intent is to define a **thought-leadership and collaboration framework** that promotes responsible, innovative use of agentic AI across the **end‑to‑end pharmaceutical biostatistics lifecycle**.

Key objectives included:

* Aligning on **scope and mission**

* Discussing **governance, permissions, and open‑source strategy**

* Identifying **near‑term, low‑burden deliverables**

* Exploring **coordination with existing industry and cross‑organization efforts**

## 2. Agentic AI Philosophy and Governance

### Discussion Points

* Participants agreed that **agentic AI should not be granted broad, unsupervised permissions**, particularly in regulated biostatistical workflows.

* AI is viewed primarily as:

* An **automation enabler** for routine or structured steps

* A **drafting and ideation assistant** for complex or fuzzy logic problems

* **Human-in-the-loop review and sign-off** was repeatedly emphasized as non‑negotiable, especially for:

* Complex statistical logic

* Analysis strategy decisions

* Model selection and interpretation

### Alignment

* The group does **not** see agentic AI as replacing statisticians.

* The target is **speed, consistency, and cognitive augmentation**, not autonomy.

* Final accountability remains with human statisticians.

## 3. GitHub Repository and Collaboration Model

### Current Setup

* A GitHub repository has been created for this subteam under the working name:

**ASA Biopharm AI/ML – Agentic Workflows**

* The repository currently contains:

* Placeholder structure

* Initial README concepts

* An **Apache 2.0 license**, chosen for consistency with other pharma-adjacent open‑source efforts

### Key Discussion Points

* **Access constraints**:

* Many company enterprise GitHub accounts cannot interact with public repos.

* Practical workaround: contributors use **personal GitHub accounts**, while remaining mindful of company policies.

* **Contribution model**:

* Contributors retain control over what they push.

* Sensitive or proprietary ideas must be reviewed before public posting.

* **Compliance awareness**:

* Public-facing outputs may still require internal company review (analogous to publication or statistics governance reviews).

### Decisions

* Apache 2.0 remains the default license for now.

* Content will be **reviewed internally within the subteam** before public posting.

* Participants will share their **GitHub usernames** to be added as collaborators.

## 4. Draft Mission Statement and Scope

### Draft Mission (Conceptual Agreement)

The group broadly agreed on a mission centered on:

* Promoting **responsible and innovative adoption of agentic AI**

* Covering the **entire biostatistics lifecycle**, including:

* Study and protocol design

* Trial monitoring

* Automated analysis

* Reporting and submission readiness

### Suggested Enhancements

* Explicitly include **regulatory interaction** (e.g., FDA meetings such as Type B / Type C).

* Acknowledge future scenarios involving **AI‑to‑AI interaction** (e.g., sponsor AI and regulator AI), while clearly preserving a **human oversight layer**.

* Emphasize collaboration with:

* Regulatory and standards bodies

* Programming and submission standards organizations

### Outcome

* Mission statement to be **revised** to:

* Broaden framing from “protocol design” to **“study design”**

* Reflect regulatory and ecosystem engagement more explicitly

## 5. Conceptual and Technical Focus Areas

### Mental Map / Coherence Engine Concept

A major conceptual idea discussed was an **AI-enabled “coherence interpretation engine”** that would:

* Create a mental map linking:

* Datasets → tables → figures → listings

* Help reviewers:

* Understand how outputs relate to each other

* Detect coherence or inconsistency across a submission package

* Potentially visualize **data lineage and propagation**

* Serve as a cognitive amplifier for interpreting large, complex submission datasets

### Group Reaction

* Broad agreement that this is a **natural AI strength**

* Viewed as especially promising for:

* End‑to‑end reasoning

* Visualization of analysis flow

* Submission quality control

## 6. Near‑Term Deliverables: White Paper Strategy

### Primary Consensus Deliverable

The group agreed that the most feasible, high‑impact short‑term deliverable is a **white paper** focusing on:

* Responsible AI use in biostatistical workflows

* Practical, non‑proprietary use cases

* Human‑in‑the‑loop design principles

* Lessons learned and future directions

### Strategic Alignment

* Strong consensus **not to work in isolation**

* Preference for a **joint industry effort** rather than parallel or duplicative papers

* Potential collaboration pathways discussed:

* ASA Biopharm Section as the formal representative body

* Existing industry initiatives and cross‑regional efforts (US and China)

### Endorsement and Visibility

* Potential alignment with:

* Industry consortia and standards communities

* Existing guideline‑producing organizations

* Goal: a **single, visible, credible industry white paper**

## 7. Open Source Contributions and Case Studies

### Discussion Points

* Open‑source contributions are encouraged but must be:

* Non‑proprietary

* Appropriately reviewed

* Potential contributions include:

* Conceptual frameworks

* Toy examples or demos

* Reusable utilities or tooling patterns

* Case studies presented at conferences (once cleared)

### Practical Examples Mentioned

* Data transformation and QC workflows

* Handling of vendor‑generated vs internal data

* Automation around reporting pipelines

* Study design benchmarks (linked to existing initiatives rather than duplicated)

### Decision

* The repository will **link out to existing benchmark efforts** where appropriate, rather than recreating them.

* Original contributions should emphasize **value‑add framing and integration**, not redundancy.

## 8. Survey Proposal: Understanding Industry Pain Points

### New Idea Introduced

A proposal was made to conduct a **focused industry survey** to understand:

* Where AI could deliver the most value across study design and analysis

* How pain points differ by company size and maturity

* Gaps between existing templates/workflows and AI‑ready processes

### Key Insights Shared

* Large-company templates are not necessarily AI‑optimal.

* Established processes may slow adaptation compared to greenfield designs.

* Smaller or newer organizations may benefit disproportionately from AI‑driven study design support.

### Outcome

* Agreement to develop a **focused pilot survey**, likely centered on study design.

* Audience could be reached via **ASA Biopharm membership channels**.

## 9. Decisions and Consensus Summary

**Agreed:**

* Human‑in‑the‑loop is essential for agentic AI in biostatistics

* GitHub will be used as the central collaboration hub

* White paper is the primary near‑term deliverable

* Efforts should align with, not duplicate, existing industry initiatives

* Open source contributions should be modular, reviewed, and non‑proprietary
