# AI Agentic Workflows for Clinical Trial Design & Reporting
**Meeting Date:** June 5, 2026  
**Meeting:** ASA Biopharm AI/ML WG Subteam Kickoff  

---

## 📝 Meeting Summary

### 1. Key Themes

#### A. AI Capabilities (Execution & Validation)
- AI can reliably:
  - Generate statistical design code and simulations
  - Reproduce analyses with clear specifications
  - Identify inconsistencies across SAP and protocol documents
- Strong confidence in AI performance when inputs are well-defined

#### B. Key Gap: Design Context
- Most challenging step remains early-stage design context:
  - Clinical landscape understanding
  - Regulatory strategy considerations
  - Prior data integration
  - Cross-functional decision-making
- Much of this knowledge is implicit and not well documented

#### C. Agreed Framework
Team aligned on splitting workflow into 3 stages:
1. **Design Context (Inputs)**
2. **Execution (Design & Simulation)**
3. **Validation (QC & Consistency Checks)**

This framework will guide survey design and AI opportunity identification.

#### D. Survey Objective
- Identify:
  - Where statisticians spend the most time
  - Key pain points and bottlenecks
  - Judgment-heavy areas less suitable for automation
  - Current AI usage patterns
- Additional focus on prompt engineering capability

#### E. Strategic Considerations
- Potential development of benchmark datasets to:
  - Evaluate AI performance
  - Serve as training data
- Open concern about loss of expert differentiation
- General agreement that participation is necessary to shape future direction

#### F. Overall Takeaway
- AI is strong in execution and validation
- AI is limited in contextual reasoning and domain-driven decision-making
- Future focus should shift toward improving design input stage
