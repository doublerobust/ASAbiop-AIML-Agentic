## 2. Key Initiative: Industry Survey on AI in Study Design

**Purpose:** Understand real friction points and opportunities for AI in clinical trial design.

### Key Design Decisions
- **Focus initially on:** Late-phase oncology trials
- **Approach:** Decompose "study design" into specific decision components rather than treating it as one broad concept
- **Collect feedback on:**
  - Where AI is most valuable
  - Where implementation barriers exist

### Implementation Approach
- Build a survey tool (web + backend form + GitHub integration)
- Iteratively refine:
  - Survey questions
  - Response options

### Distribution Strategy (important insight)
- Avoid broad, unfocused outreach (historically low response rates)
- **Instead:**
  - Use targeted distribution
  - Engage experienced statisticians and domain experts
  - Leverage company-level champions to drive participation
- **Potential channels:**
  - Internal company networks (e.g., Merck)
  - Cross-company contacts (BMS, etc.)
  - Conferences and professional networks
  - Selective LinkedIn outreach

## 4. AI Workflow Strategy Discussion

**Key Insight:** The main challenge is NOT technology, but domain understanding and workflow integration.

### Observations
- AI tools (LLMs, coding agents) are already powerful
- Bottlenecks include:
  - Translating business needs into AI workflows
  - Integrating into regulated environments
  - Scaling from prototype → production

---

## 5. Prototype vs Production Gap (Critical Theme)

### Current State
Individuals can build useful prototypes/tools. However, enterprise deployment is difficult.

### Key Challenges
- Maintenance and scalability
- Integration with enterprise systems
- Usability across diverse users
- Compliance / regulated environment constraints

### Conclusion
Production requires:
- Dedicated engineering support
- Sustained investment (not one-off effort)

---

## 6. Build vs External Partnership Debate

### Options Discussed
- Internal development
- External (Big Tech / vendors) collaboration
- Hybrid / embedded engineering models

### Key Trade-offs
| | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| **External partners** | Strong engineering capability | Limited domain understanding initially |
| **Internal teams** | Strong business/domain expertise | Limited engineering scalability |

### Emerging Consensus
- Domain knowledge is the primary bottleneck
- Likely need: Internal domain-driven design + External support for scaling (selectively)

---

## 7. Global / China Landscape Insight

Noted strong activity in China:
- National policy pushing health data infrastructure
- Pharma companies collaborating closely with tech firms
- Alternative model: Build internal AI/engineering teams with domain expertise (rather than fully outsourcing)

---

## 8. Training & Adoption Challenge (Major Risk)

### Observations
- Very low adoption of:
  - AI coding tools (e.g., Codex)
  - Knowledge tools (NotebookLM, etc.)
- Even among interested users, engagement and completion remain low

### Key Insight
Adoption barrier is **behavioral and cultural**, not technical.

### Implication
Cannot rely on training everyone to be AI experts.
Instead focus on:
- Building robust workflows and systems
- Embedding AI into processes

---

## 9. Strategic Direction (Emerging Consensus)

**Prioritize:**
- Workflow architecture
- Process design
- End-to-end systems

**De-emphasize:**
- Individual-level AI expertise training
