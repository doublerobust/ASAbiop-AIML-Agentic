## README.md: ASA Biopharmaceutical Section AI/ML Working Group

### Workstream: Agentic AI in Clinical Development

---

## 🌟 Mission Statement

The mission of this workstream is to promote the **responsible and innovative adoption** of Agentic AI workflows within the field of pharmaceutical biostatistics. We focus on the entire lifecycle of clinical research, including the design, setup, monitoring, and ultimately analysis and reporting of clinical trials.

By bridging the gap between cutting-edge AI research and rigorous biostatistical requirements, we aim to establish best practices that ensure safety, transparency, and statistical integrity.

---

## 🚀 Overview

Agentic AI represents a shift from passive models to autonomous or semi-autonomous "agents" capable of using tools, reasoning through complex tasks, and collaborating to solve multi-step problems. In the context of clinical development, these workflows have the potential to:

* **Automate Protocol Design:** Utilizing historical data and simulation agents to optimize study parameters.
* **Enhance Trial Monitoring:** Deploying agents to identify data trends or safety signals in real-time.
* **Streamline Analysis & Reporting:** Developing "skills" for agents to produce TLF mockups from protocol/SAP, generate programming specs for ADaM setups, use synthetic data to conduct dry-runs, and eventually produce TLFs (Tables, Listings, and Figures) and draft clinical study reports (CSRs) with human-in-the-loop validation.
* **AI-enabled Cohereance and Interpretation Engine** Developing a topological "mental map" system that dynamically interconnects and validates the entire suite of study tables, listings, and figures to guide reviewers through a coherent, cross-referenced interpretation of clinical trial results.
* **Standardize Workflows:** Creating reproducible agentic frameworks for CDISC data transformations and statistical programming.

---

## 👥 Our Team

This workstream is composed of a dedicated group of thought leaders, biostatisticians, and AI engineers active in the pharmaceutical and biotechnology industry. Our members contribute expertise in:

* **Biostatistical Methodology:** Ensuring AI-generated outputs meet regulatory standards and follow sound statistical principles.
* **Agentic Frameworks:** Designing multi-agent systems using technologies such as AutoGen, LangGraph, or CrewAI.
* **Clinical Operations:** Integrating AI into the practical realities of trial management and data privacy.

---

## 🛠 Active Projects & Workflows

* **Agentic Skill Development:** Building a library of specialized tools (e.g., sample size calculators, R-programming agents) that can be orchestrated by a central LLM.
* **Validation Frameworks:** Developing benchmarks to test the reliability and accuracy of AI agents in a "GXP" compliant environment.
* **White Papers & Guidance:** Drafting industry-standard recommendations for the "Responsible AI" lifecycle in biostatistics.

---

## 📂 Repository Structure

* `/docs`: White papers, meeting notes, and regulatory guidance summaries.
* `/examples`: Conceptual agentic workflows and "Hello World" examples for clinical tasks.
* `/tools`: Shared utility scripts and API wrappers for common biostatistical libraries.
* `/benchmarks`: Data sets and scripts for testing agent performance.
